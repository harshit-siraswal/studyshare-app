# StudyShare Mobile Design System

## Product context
- StudyShare is a college-first mobile app for discovering resources, notices, rooms, AI study tools, and attendance workflows.
- The onboarding experience is the first-run layer before college selection and login, so it should sell the product quickly, feel premium, and make the value of the app obvious without reading dense copy.
- The visual tone should feel modern, minimal, and focused rather than playful or loud.

## Brand direction
- Preserve the StudyShare blue brand family centered on `#2563EB`.
- Prefer dark-first presentation for onboarding and hero moments, with support for light mode fallbacks.
- Typography stays in `Inter` for body and UI labels.
- Visual language should feel native to a polished AI-era productivity app: controlled contrast, strong spacing, soft glass panels, restrained highlights, and calm motion.

## Typography
- Headline style: high-contrast, compact line-height, weight `700-800`.
- Body copy: `Inter`, readable, slightly relaxed line height.
- Supporting labels: small uppercase or compact pill labels with medium weight.

## Layout rules
- Onboarding should use full-bleed hero surfaces rather than small centered icon cards.
- Keep one strong focal composition per page.
- Bottom CTA region should remain clear and thumb-friendly.
- The page should feel spacious, with generous margins and only a few high-value UI elements per page.

## Motion rules
- Motion should be layered and calm: floating background orbs, parallax cards, soft scale-ins, and slow ambient movement.
- Avoid noisy particle storms, aggressive rotations, or rainbow gradients.
- CTA and page transitions should feel direct and responsive.

## Component rules
- Cards: 20-28 px radius with subtle border and low-opacity fill.
- Pills: full radius, compact, with thin border and muted iconography.
- Progress indicators: minimal and integrated into the bottom action area.
- Hero visuals can use stacked cards, floating surfaces, and subtle glow fields.

## Onboarding-specific constraints
- The onboarding should communicate StudyShare's core value areas: resources, rooms, campus updates, and focused study support.
- The design should feel more premium and memorable than the current icon-and-copy pager.
- Do not introduce unrelated colors or whimsical illustration styles that clash with the app's serious study/productivity positioning.
